THE GRAND TIMELINE

Journey from the dawn of Pharaonic dynamic unifications to the modern era of the Egyptian Republic.

c. 3100 BCE

King Narmer (Menes)

Early Dynastic Period

The legendary king who united Upper and Lower Egypt, establishing the First Dynasty and the capital of Memphis.

c. 2670 BCE

King Djoser

Old Kingdom

Commissioned the famous Step Pyramid at Saqqara, the world's first monumental stone structure.

c. 2589 BCE

King Khufu

Old Kingdom

Builder of the Great Pyramid of Giza, the supreme architectural masterpiece and ancient wonder.

c. 2558 BCE

King Khafre

Old Kingdom

Constructed the second largest Giza pyramid and carved the colossal Sphinx as an eternal guardian.

c. 2532 BCE

King Menkaure

Old Kingdom

Completed the Giza trio by constructing the third pyramid, famed for its elegant triad statues.

1478 – 1458 BCE

Queen Hatshepsut

New Kingdom

One of Egypt's most successful female pharaohs, expanding trade and constructing Deir el-Bahari temple.

1479 – 1425 BCE

Thutmose III

New Kingdom

A supreme military general who created Egypt's largest empire through seventeen brilliant campaigns.

1388 – 1351 BCE

Amenhotep III

New Kingdom

Ruled during a golden age of unprecedented wealth, cultural diplomacy, and monumental art expansion.

1351 – 1334 BCE

Akhenaten

New Kingdom

Triggered a religious revolution by introducing monotheistic worship centered on the sun disc, Aten.

1332 – 1323 BCE

Tutankhamun

New Kingdom

The boy king who restored the traditional gods and whose intact golden tomb stunned the modern world.

1319 – 1292 BCE

Horemheb

New Kingdom

The general-turned-pharaoh who stabilized the nation, crushed corruption, and reformed the legal codes.

1290 – 1279 BCE

Seti I

New Kingdom

Restored Egypt's foreign prestige and built the breathtaking Hypostyle Hall at Karnak temple.

1279 – 1213 BCE

Ramesses II

New Kingdom

Ramesses the Great; celebrated warrior who signed the world's first peace treaty and carved Abu Simbel.

1186 – 1155 BCE

Ramesses III

New Kingdom

The last great warrior pharaoh, famously defending Egypt against the massive invasions of the Sea Peoples.

360 – 342 BCE

Nectanebo II

Late Period

The final native Egyptian pharaoh of antiquity before the country fell to persistent foreign conquest.

525 BCE

Cambyses II (Persian Conquest)

Achaemenid Empire

The Persian king who defeated Pharaoh Psamtik III, turning Egypt into a province of the Persian Empire.

332 BCE

Alexander the Great

Macedonian Empire

Liberated Egypt from Persian rule, was declared a son of Amun, and laid the foundations of Alexandria.

305 – 282 BCE

Ptolemy I Soter

Ptolemaic Kingdom

General of Alexander who crowned himself king, initiating the famous Greek rule over the Egyptian state.

51 – 30 BCE

Cleopatra VII

Ptolemaic Kingdom

The iconic, brilliant final ruler of the Ptolemaic dynasty whose dramatic reign led to the Roman conquest.

30 BCE

Augustus Caesar

Roman Empire

Annexed Egypt directly into the Roman Empire as its most prized and highly guarded agricultural province.

641 CE

Amr ibn al-As

Rashidun Caliphate

Led the Arab-Islamic opening of Egypt, ending Byzantine dominion and founding the new capital of Fustat.

868 – 884 CE

Ahmad ibn Tulun

Tulunid Dynasty

Founded the Tulunid dynasty, achieving Egypt's first independent statehood from the central Abbasid Caliphate.

969 CE

Gawhar al-Siqilli

Fatimid Caliphate

The brilliant general who conquered Egypt and built the historic walls of Cairo and the sacred Al-Azhar Mosque.

973 – 975 CE

Al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah

Fatimid Caliphate

The first Fatimid caliph to reside in Egypt, officially shifting the seat of the empire to Cairo.

1171 – 1193 CE

Salah ad-Din (Saladin)

Ayyubid Dynasty

Founded the Ayyubid dynasty, fortified Cairo with its famous Citadel, and defended the Levant against Crusaders.

1259 – 1260 CE

Saif ad-Din Qutuz

Mamluk Sultanate

The heroic Sultan who decisively crushed the seemingly unstoppable Mongol armies at the Battle of Ain Jalut.

1260 – 1277 CE

Al-Zahir Baibars

Mamluk Sultanate

A brilliant administrator and commander who institutionalized the dominant golden era of the Mamluk Empire.

1501 – 1516 CE

Sultan Qansuh al-Ghawri

Mamluk Sultanate

Reconstructed massive civic projects before dying in epic combat defending Syria from Ottoman annexation.

1517 CE

Sultan Selim I

Ottoman Empire

Defeated the Mamluks at Ridaniya, transforming Egypt into an official administrative Ottoman Eyalet.

1805 – 1848 CE

Muhammad Ali Pasha

Alawiyya Dynasty

The founder of Modern Egypt; completely restructured the military, educational, economic, and industrial systems.

1848 CE

Ibrahim Pasha

Alawiyya Dynasty

A highly accomplished military general who expanded regional campaigns before briefly securing the throne.

1854 – 1863 CE

Sa'id Pasha

Alawiyya Dynasty

Signed the critical initial concession granting the permission to excavate and build the Suez Canal.

1863 – 1879 CE

Khedive Ismail

Alawiyya Dynasty

Modernized Cairo dramatically, inaugurated the Suez Canal, and aggressively expanded educational frameworks.

1914 – 1917 CE

Sultan Hussein Kamel

Alawiyya Dynasty

Declared Sultan during World War I as Great Britain declared an official unilateral Protectorate over Egypt.

1917 – 1936 CE

King Fuad I

Alawiyya Dynasty

Transformed his title from Sultan to King in 1922 following the historic nominal unilateral independence declaration.

1936 – 1952 CE

King Farouk I

Alawiyya Dynasty

The final absolute reigning monarch of Egypt, whose administration was overthrown by the 1952 Revolution.

1953 – 1954 CE

Mohamed Naguib

Egyptian Republic

The very first official President of Egypt following the formal abolition of the hereditary monarchy system.

1954 – 1970 CE

Gamal Abdel Nasser

Egyptian Republic

Nationalized the Suez Canal, constructed the High Dam, and spearheaded the global Pan-Arab movement.

1970 – 1981 CE

Anwar Sadat

Egyptian Republic

Led the historic 1973 October War victory, reassigned the open economy, and signed the historic Camp David Accord.

1981 – 2011 CE

Hosni Mubarak

Egyptian Republic

Maintained regional political stability and focused heavily on vast domestic infrastructure expansions over three decades.

2012 – 2013 CE

Mohamed Morsi

Egyptian Republic

Served as president for one year following elections, before massive public protests led to his political removal.

2013 – 2014 CE

Adly Mansour

Egyptian Republic

The Chief Justice of the Supreme Constitutional Court who served as transitional president during a critical interim period.

2014 – Present

Abdel Fattah El-Sisi

Egyptian Republic

Focuses on comprehensive national structural updates, constructing the New Administrative Capital, and massive economic corridors.
